A proposal for establishing a NAMED ENDOWMENT may be made by any person or institution.

I  FORMATION OF NAMED ENDOWMENT.
  1. The donor may specify the name of the endowment.

  2. A named endowment can be instituted with a minimum commitment or payment of US $ 25,000 to The Rotary Foundation.

    A contact person initiates the proposal and forwards the information to RRFC / MGA / consultant at RISAO

    I) Proposal - An Endowed fund proposal is given to prospective donors explaining what his gift is capable of doing. I prepare the proposal based on the interest of the donor. The following background information of the prospect is required for the donor

    • Area of interest

    • What program/ cause does he wish to support

    • Past history of contribution to rotary

    • How does he want the gift to be used in Rotary

    • Rotary ID if any

    II) Cover Letter- Along with the proposal a cover letter is sent which is signed by the contact person ,the Major gift advisor or any concerned person who is interacting with the prospective donor. To prepare the letter the following Information is required

    • name of the endowment

    • Name and address, tel number

    • designation in the company

    • Amount of contribution

    The proposal should be handed over in person to the donor.

  3. Upon receipt of the minimum sum of US $ 25,000 an agreement (Endowed fund form No.2) is signed by the donor and the General Secretary of Rotary International and a NAMED ENDOWMENT is established. This form will be initiated by The Rotary Foundation. A signed copy of the form is given to the donor.

  4. The minimum amount of US $ 25,000 may be contributed over a 3 year period provided the first installment is a minimum of US $ 8,333.

    The second installment should complete a contribution of minimum US $ 16,666.

    The third installment should reach the minimum contribution of US$ 25,000.

  5. Where the sums are contributed in installments, the donor will sign two agreements (ENDOWED FUND FORM and a PLEDGE FORM. These forms will be initiated by The Rotary Foundation.

    The NAMED ENDOWMENT will be established upon receipt of the minimum contribution of US $ 25,000 to this fund.

II  DISPOSITION OF EARNINGS

  • An endowed gift is a gift made to the Permanent Fund. The principal of an endowed fund gift is never spent, while a portion of the income is made available for specific Rotary programs as determined by the donor

  • The spendable earning of the endowment will come as an income to the District as DDF.

  • The annual earnings ( only a portion determined by the Foundation Trustees) from the named endowment would be used to encourage and fund humanitarian projects within your district.

  • For Contribution designated PF-Share, currently 50% of spendable earnings would be returned to the district as DDF and be available to fund matching grant projects, 50% placed in the Foundations World Fund available to the Trustees to "match the district's requests" when submitted.

  • If the donor desires that this income be used for a specified purpose, the arrangement needs to be formalized at the District level. (The District and donor may pass a resolution at the District conference so that this amount will be used in future for the specified purpose).

  • The income will be received by the District as part of the share cycle.

III  FAQ

  1. Q. I have already contributed monies to TRF. Can I top up these contributions to establish a named endowment.

    A. No. Named endowments need to be separately pledged and committed. Earlier contribution to APF / Permanent fund or Major donors does not count for Named endowment establishment.

  2. Q. What is the disposition of the contribution if the targeted contribution of US$ 25,000 is not reached within 3 years.

    A. The contribution will be taken into the Permanent Fund in the name of the donor.

  3. Q. I earn Paul Harris Fellow recognition points for the “named endowment” contribution.

    A. No. this contribution is credited to the permanent fund.

  4. Q. Am I eligible for Major donor recognition with this contribution to Named endowment.

    A. Yes. You will be eligible for appropriate Major Donor recognition.

  5. Q. My club wants to institute an endowment in the name of the club.

    A. No. A club cannot form an endowment.

  6. Q. What is the expectation of the earning.How much money will the District receive?

    A .Currently the rate of interest as determined by the trustees is 5% effective 1 July 2007
