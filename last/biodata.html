Brief Biography                                                     Photography

Know Krishnan V. Chari

A Rotarian since 1983. Chari was the ‘caring’ District Governor 1997-98 of RI District 3230.Chari is the Regional Rotary Foundation Coordinator for Zone 6, comprising countries in South Asia since July 2005. This is the second year of his term. In Chari’s first year as RRFC, the Annual program fund contribution of Zone 6 increased by 28% over the previous year to over US$ 2.3 million. In zone 5 and 6A - India crossed the US $ 4 million mark of total contributions to The Rotary Foundation.

Sixty two years old, Chari has his education in Mumbai and U.K. and is a qualified Engineer. He worked in U.K. for 9 years with Vickers Ltd and IBM before returning to India. In Mumbai he worked with IBM, Crompton and Tatas in senior management positions. Settling down in Chennai in 1983, he is currently a distributor for “The Hindu” one of India’s national newspapers and is associated with agencies for paper products, gifts and represents many firms of Mumbai and Delhi.

In Rotary, Chari was the President of Rotary Club of Meenambakkam in 1987-88. Chari was GSR of Rotary Clubs of Madras Temple City and Madras Shakthi, which pioneered the induction of women into Rotary. Some of his pet projects are the Rotary Computer centers for imparting computer education to students, anti smoking project and the Book bank. What is Chari proud of as a Governor?

The PRE PRIMARY EDUCATION PROGRAMME – starting of KG classes on Corporation Schools. Chennai has become a landmark. This was followed up with an enrolment drive to promote school attendance.

THE GOLDEN RUNto celebrate 50th Anniversary of Indian independence saw 8000 children in a run of celebration from Marina beach to Island Grounds at Chennai, a distance of 6kms.

Light up the face of child was the invitation to 6000 orphans, many of them handicapped to share the joy of Diwali a festival with mammoth entertainment, cracker show and a dinner that received wide accolades from the public and the media.

MEAL DAYprogramme involved all the Rotary Clubs in providing meal everyday in one or more orphanages / senior citizen homes / childcare centers under the banner of Rotary Hunger alleviation programme. It is encouraging to note that this programme continues even after the year is over.

A FUNCTIONAL LITERACY AND VOCATIONAL SKILLSTrust is another programme that build upon the success of future years on top of providing training and employment to over 1100 youth during 1997-98,

DISCON 98 the District Conference was a great success with 4230 delegates and top quality programmes and entertainment.

Chari raised over US $ 120,000 as a Governor for The Rotary Foundation in addition to over 50 Rotary Foundation benefactors enrolled then. Many matching grants were implemented by the clubs. It can be truly said that the lay public took notice of Rotary.

He led the GSE team to Philippines in 1992.

He was the District Trainer in 1999-2000.

He was the Chief District Counsellor in 2001-02 as also chairperson of District Grants subcommittee. Chennai Olympiad and Chennai Marathon were his brain child ideas in this year.

Chari represented the RI President at the District Conference of District 3190 in 2002.

Chari received appreciation from all Rotarians as the Chair District Awards in 2005 – 06.

Chari took over as editor of “ROTARY NEWS” Magazine in October 1998, and made this a popular magazine. To his credit he built up the corpus of Rotary News Trust, which has established “Rotary Samachar Resource Centre” to provide Rotary information and to undertake research activities. The Rotary News Trust acquired its own premises costing Rupees 1.68 crores during November 2002. The whole effort has been due to Chari. 50th Year of Rotary News was celebrated in a grand fashion in December 2002. The “Last Page” column of Rotary News authored by Chari has many avid readers. Chari stepped down from the editorship in September 2005 after a tenure of seven years.

Chari is married to Dr. Nithya. Nithya is a teacher and now looks after their business interest. Nithya and Chari are major donors and benefactors. Their grandson is also a Paul Harris fellow as is their daughter and son-in-law.

With his vast experience in Rotary affairs at all levels, Chari has acquired a good knowledge of Rotary procedures and guidelines and is consulted by many Rotary Clubs and districts.

Chari has been actively associated and held key assignments in many national level programmes of Rotary in India such as Population Summit,(2000) Polioplus Summit (2003) and Presidential Celebration on Literacy (Secretary 2004 – 05) besides Zonal Institutes.

Chari was a member of the Chicago Convention Promotion Committee and the registration for RI Zones 5 & 6 exceeded the target of 2500.

Chari is a professional, a pathfinder, entrepreneur, a committed Rotarian and above all a fine and lovable person.
